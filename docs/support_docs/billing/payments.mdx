## Adding a Payment Method

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Billing Settings">
    Settings > **Billing** > **Payment Methods**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add New Payment Method">
    Click **Add Payment Method**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Payment Details">
    Fill in card number, expiration, CVC, and billing address
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. Card is validated and stored securely.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### What We Store

- Last 4 digits of card
- Card brand (Visa, Mastercard, etc.)
- Expiration date
- Billing address

Your full card number is never stored on our servers. Stripe handles all sensitive data.

---

## Updating Your Payment Method

If your card expires or you want to switch cards:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Payment Methods">
    Settings > **Billing** > **Payment Methods**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Current Method">
    Click on the card you want to update
  </Step>

  <Step title="Update Details">
    Change expiration date, billing address, or add new card
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set as Default">
    If adding new card, set it as default for future charges
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  If you have an active subscription, the new payment method is charged on your next billing date.
</Info>

---

## Setting a Default Payment Method

If you have multiple cards saved, set which one to use:

1. Settings > **Billing** > **Payment Methods**
2. Hover over the card you want as default
3. Click **Set as Default**
4. Future charges use this card

---

## Removing a Payment Method

Delete old or expired cards:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Payment Methods">
    Settings > **Billing** > **Payment Methods**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Card to Remove">
    Click on the card you want to delete
  </Step>

  <Step title="Delete">
    Click **Remove Payment Method** > Confirm
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  You can't remove the default payment method if you have an active Pro subscription. Add a new card and set it as default first.
</Warning>

## Payment Security

### How We Keep Your Data Safe

- **PCI DSS Compliant** - Stripe is Level 1 PCI certified (highest level)
- **Tokenization** - Card details replaced with secure tokens
- **Encryption** - All data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3)
- **No Storage** - We never store full card numbers
- **3D Secure** - Supported for extra fraud protection

### What You See vs What We See

**You See:**
- Full card number when entering
- All card details in Stripe checkout

**We See:**
- Last 4 digits only
- Card brand (Visa, etc.)
- Token ID (like `card_abc123xyz`)

This means even if our database is compromised, your payment info is safe.

## Failed Payments

### Why Payments Fail

Common reasons:

- Insufficient funds
- Expired card
- Incorrect CVV/CVC
- Bank decline (fraud detection)
- Incorrect billing address
- Card doesn't support recurring charges

### What Happens When Payment Fails

1. **Immediate Email** - We notify you of failed payment
2. **Retry #1** - Automatic retry after 3 days
3. **Retry #2** - Another retry after 5 days
4. **Retry #3** - Final retry after 7 days
5. **Downgrade** - If all retries fail, account moves to Free plan

Your data is not deleted. Update payment method anytime to restore Pro access.

### Fixing Failed Payments

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check Email">
    We send details about why payment failed
  </Step>

  <Step title="Update Payment Method">
    Settings > **Billing** > **Payment Methods** > Add new card
  </Step>

  <Step title="Retry Charge">
    We automatically retry within 24 hours of updating card
  </Step>

  <Step title="Restore Access">
    Pro features restored immediately after successful charge
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Need Manual Retry?** Email billing@govibey.com

## International Payments

### Supported Countries

## Refunds

### Refund to Payment Method

All refunds go back to the original payment method used.

- **Credit card:** 5-10 business days
- **Debit card:** 5-10 business days
- **Apple Pay/Google Pay:** 5-10 business days

Timing depends on your bank.

### 7-Day Money-Back Guarantee

First-time Pro subscribers get full refund if canceled within 7 days.

**To Request:**
1. Email billing@govibey.com with subject "Refund Request"
2. Include account email
3. We process within 1-2 business days
4. Refund arrives in 5-10 days

### Prorated Refunds (Annual Plans)

Cancel annual plan after 7 days? You get prorated refund for unused months.

**Example:**
- You paid $1,497 for annual plan
- You cancel after 4 months
- You get refund for 8 unused months: $998

## Payment FAQs

**Can I pay with PayPal?**
Not yet. Credit/debit cards, Apple Pay, and Google Pay only. PayPal coming Q1 2026.

**Do you accept bank transfers (ACH/Wire)?**
Enterprise plans only. Email enterprise@govibey.com.

**Can I pay with cryptocurrency?**
Not currently. No plans to add crypto payments.

**Why was I charged twice?**
Likely a pending charge that will disappear in 3-5 days. If both charges settle, email billing@govibey.com.

**Can I get an invoice before payment?**
For Enterprise plans with PO requirements, yes. Email billing@govibey.com.

**How do I update my billing email?**
Settings > **Billing** > **Billing Email** > Enter new email. Invoices will be sent there.

**What's the charge descriptor on my statement?**
You'll see "VIBEOS.COM" or "VIBEY" on your bank statement.

**Can I use prepaid cards?**
Yes, if they support recurring billing. Some prepaid cards don't allow subscriptions.

**Is there a minimum commitment?**
No. Cancel anytime. No long-term contracts.

## Payment Best Practices

### Keep Your Card Updated

Set calendar reminder 1 month before card expires. Update before expiration to avoid service interruption.

### Use Backup Payment Method

Add secondary card to automatically charge if primary fails.

**To Add Backup:**
1. Add second payment method
2. It stays on file as backup
3. Auto-charged if primary fails

### Monitor Billing Emails

Always read billing emails. They contain:
- Upcoming charge notices (3 days before)
- Successful payment confirmations
- Failed payment alerts

### Check Bank for Declines

If payment fails, check with your bank:
- Is card active?
- Sufficient funds?
- Is recurring billing enabled?
- Is international billing blocked?
